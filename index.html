Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about property investment calculators and UK investing.

A good rental yield depends on the property type and location. In the UK, gross yields of 5-8% are considered good for residential buy-to-let. Northern cities like Manchester, Liverpool, and Glasgow often yield 6-8%, while London averages 3-4%. Our calculator helps you compare both gross and net yield so you see the real return after all costs.
BRRR stands for Buy-Refurbish-Refinance-Rent. You buy a property below market value, refurbish it to increase value, refinance to pull your capital back out (often 75% of the new value), then rent it for ongoing cash flow. The goal is to own a cash-flowing property with little to none of your own money left in the deal. Use our BRRR calculator to see if a deal stacks up before you commit.
Net rental yield = (Annual Rental Income − Annual Costs) ÷ Property Purchase Price × 100. Annual costs include mortgage interest, letting agent fees (8-15%), insurance (typically £150-300/yr), maintenance reserve (1% of property value), and void periods. Our calculator factors all of these in so you see the true return.

Most UK buy-to-let mortgages require a 25% deposit, though some lenders offer 20% for experienced investors or limited company purchases. For a £200,000 property, that's £50,000 minimum. You'll also need £7,000-£10,000 for stamp duty, legal fees, and surveys. Use our deal analyser to factor all these costs into your calculation.
