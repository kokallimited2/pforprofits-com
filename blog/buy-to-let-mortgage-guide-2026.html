Buy-to-Let Mortgage Guide 2026

Everything you need to know about BTL mortgages in 2026: rates, deposits, lender criteria, and how to get the best deal for your property investment.

By Ateeq Yousif · May 14, 2026 · 11 min read

Getting the right buy-to-let mortgage is often the difference between a profitable property and one that drains your cash flow. In 2026, the mortgage landscape is shaped by interest rates that remain elevated compared to the ultra-low environment of 2020-2022, but have stabilised enough for confident planning. This guide covers everything you need to know — from deposit requirements and interest rates to lender stress tests and portfolio criteria.

Current BTL Interest Rates (May 2026)

As of May 2026, the buy-to-let mortgage market offers the following indicative rates:

60% LTV (40% deposit): 3.8% – 4.3% — The best rates available. Suitable for investors with significant equity or cash reserves. These are typically 2 and 5-year fixed products.

65% LTV (35% deposit): 4.0% – 4.5% — The sweet spot for many portfolio landlords. Good rates with manageable deposit requirements.

75% LTV (25% deposit): 4.5% – 5.2% — The most common product for new investors. Higher rates but more accessible deposit-wise.

80% LTV (20% deposit): 5.0% – 5.8% — Limited availability. Usually requires a strong application with excellent credit history and proven rental experience.

All rates above are for interest-only repayment, which is standard for BTL mortgages. If you choose a repayment mortgage (paying down capital), expect rates 0.5-1% higher but you will own the property outright at the end of the term.

Deposit Requirements: 25% vs 20%

The standard BTL deposit requirement in 2026 is 25%. This has been the market norm since the post-2016 regulatory changes and has not shifted significantly despite interest rate movements.

25% deposit: Available across all mainstream lenders. Requires £50,000 on a £200,000 property. Standard rates apply.

20% deposit: Available from a smaller pool of lenders. Requires £40,000 on a £200,000 property. Rates are typically 0.5% higher. Some lenders restrict 20% deposit products to limited company purchases or experienced landlords with existing portfolios.

40% deposit (or more): Puts you in the best rate bracket. If you are buying with cash and using a mortgage to release equity later, the BRRR strategy becomes particularly attractive at lower LTVs.

Types of BTL Mortgage Products

Interest-Only Mortgages

By far the most popular BTL product. You pay interest only each month and the capital is repaid when you sell the property or at the end of the term. This maximises monthly cash flow. Example: £150,000 mortgage at 4.5% = £562.50 per month interest-only. The same loan on a repayment basis would be approximately £835 per month over a 25-year term.

Fixed-Rate Mortgages

The most common choice in 2026. Fix your rate for 2, 3, or 5 years. A 5-year fix provides payment certainty and protects against further rate rises, which is valuable in the current environment. Two-year fixes offer lower initial rates but carry refinancing risk.

Tracker Mortgages

Variable rate products that track the Bank of England base rate plus a margin. Currently less popular given base rate volatility. May become attractive if rates begin to fall later in 2026 or 2027.

Bridging Loans

Short-term finance (6-18 months) for BRRR deals, auctions, or properties needing renovation. Rates are higher (7-12% APR) but the product is for a specific purpose and is repaid when the property is refinanced onto a standard BTL mortgage.

Lender Criteria and Stress Tests

Lenders assess BTL applications differently from residential mortgages. The key criteria in 2026:

Rental coverage ratio: Most lenders require the monthly rent to cover the mortgage payment by at least 125-145% at a stress rate of 5.5-6.0%. For example, a £600 monthly mortgage payment requires rent of at least £750-£870 per month. Lower LTVs sometimes get more favourable coverage ratios.

Minimum income: Many lenders require you to earn at least £25,000-£40,000 per year from non-rental sources, especially for your first BTL mortgage. Portfolio landlords may have different criteria.

Credit history: A clean credit record is essential. Lenders will check for missed payments, defaults, and CCJs. Any adverse credit significantly reduces the pool of available products.

Property criteria: Minimum EPC rating of C (or D for some lenders, but this is narrowing). Minimum property value of £50,000-£75,000. Some lenders have maximum property age limits for new mortgages.

Age limits: Most BTL mortgages must be taken out before age 70-75, and the term must end before age 75-85 depending on the lender.

Limited Company BTL Mortgages

Limited company (SPV) BTL mortgages remain popular despite slightly higher rates. The key advantage is tax: mortgage interest is fully deductible against rental income at the corporate tax rate (19-25%), whereas individual landlords face Section 24 restrictions that limit interest relief to the basic rate of tax.

Setting up a Special Purpose Vehicle is straightforward. You register a limited company with Companies House (SIC code 68209 — Other letting and operating of own or leased real estate) and open a business bank account. The company purchases and owns the properties.

Rates for SPV BTL mortgages are typically 0.25-0.5% higher than personal BTL products. However, for higher-rate and additional-rate taxpayers, the overall tax saving usually outweighs the rate difference.

Portfolio Landlord Criteria

If you own four or more buy-to-let properties, lenders classify you as a portfolio landlord. This triggers more stringent underwriting:

  • Full portfolio review: all properties, their values, outstanding mortgages, and rental income.
  • Contingent liability assessment: the lender checks cross-collateralisation between properties.
  • Interest coverage ratio applied across the entire portfolio, not per-property.
  • Many high street lenders limit portfolio lending to 10-20 properties. Specialist lenders go higher.

How to Get the Best BTL Mortgage Deal

1. Use a specialist broker. BTL mortgages are more complex than residential. A whole-of-market broker who specialises in BTL can access products you will not find on comparison sites. The broker fee (£500-£1,000) is usually worth it for the rate improvement alone.

2. Improve your property's EPC rating. With minimum C ratings becoming standard, an EPC D or E property will significantly limit your mortgage options. Upgrading insulation, heating, and glazing before applying can open up better products.

3. Increase your deposit. Moving from 75% to 65% LTV typically saves 0.5-0.7% on the interest rate. On a £150,000 mortgage, that is £750-£1,050 per year saved — which often beats the returns you would get investing the extra deposit elsewhere.

4. Fix for longer. In the current rate environment, a 5-year fix provides certainty and protects against further rate rises. The rate difference between 2-year and 5-year fixes has narrowed, making longer fixes more attractive.

5. Structure your portfolio properly. Consider whether personal ownership or an SPV limited company works better for your tax situation. The right structure can save thousands per year in tax on a multi-property portfolio.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I get a BTL mortgage if I already own my home? Yes. BTL mortgages are available to homeowners. Lenders will typically consider your existing residential mortgage commitments when assessing affordability. Having equity in your own home is generally seen as positive.

What happens at the end of a fixed-rate BTL mortgage? You can remortgage to a new product with the same or a different lender, or move onto the lender's standard variable rate (SVR). SVRs are typically 6-8%, so remortgaging is almost always the better choice.

Can I use a BTL mortgage for a property I want to live in? No. BTL mortgages are specifically for rental properties. Living in a BTL-mortgaged property breaches the terms and conditions. You need a residential mortgage for your own home.

How long does a BTL mortgage application take? Typically 4-8 weeks from application to completion. Straightforward applications with good documentation can complete in 3-4 weeks. Portfolio landlord applications may take 8-12 weeks.

Are BTL mortgages harder to get in 2026? Compared to 2021-2022, yes. Lenders have tightened criteria, particularly around rental coverage ratios and stress testing. However, a well-prepared application with a 25% deposit, clean credit, and professional advice should still succeed.
